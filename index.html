About Me

I have always been passionate about knowledge organization, preservation, and accessibility. My academic journey has equipped me with a strong foundation in library management and information services, having earned both a Diploma and Bachelor of Library and Information Studies acquired from the Natinal University of Lesotho. During my studies, I gained valuable hands-on experience through experiential learning at Mangoaela Library and ICT in Lesotho College of Education, where I was attached for practical training. This opportunity deepened my understanding of how libraries and archives operate while strengthening my skills in managing information resources effectively.

Beyond traditional library services, I have cultivated a strong interest in digital literacy and website development, particularly as tools for improving information access. This gave me understanding that by integrating technology into information management, the gaps in access can be easiy bridged and more nclusive digital spaces can be created for learning and research. My passion for advancing professional growth in the field led me to conduct research on training and professional development programs for librarians. I explored how these programs enhance librarians' skills and improve their ability to meet the evolving needs of library users. This research allowed me to critically assess the effectiveness of existing training initiatives and propose recommendations for improvement, ensuring that librarians are equipped to navigate the dynamic information landscape.

Moreover, I also conducted case study on special groups at the State Library of Lesotho, where I explored how national and public libraries serve marginalized communities. This research aimed to identify areas for improvement in library services to ensure they better meet the needs of diverse user populations. Through this study, I contributed to the ongoing conversation on accessibility and inclusivity in library services.

Furthermore, I worked at Morija Museum and Archives as Assistant Archivist after acquiring my Diploma, where I was responsible for sorting and filing archival materials, preparing them for digitization, and ultimately digitizing them. This project played a vital role in preserving historical records and enhancing their accessibility for researchers and the public. Through this experience, I developed expertise in digital archiving, metadata creation, and handling delicate archival materials with care.

. .

At Morija Museum and Archives, I was deeply involved in this digitization project, ensuring that valuable documents were carefully handled and converted into digital formats for future use.The first steps I was involved in were sorting and filing archival materials. This required organizing documents based on their historical significance, condition, and classification, ensuring they were properly cataloged before the digitization process began. Attention to detail was essential in maintaining the integrity of these records. The digitization itself involved scanning or photographing documents using high-resolution equipment, such as cameras, scanners and software for storage, converting them into digital formats while preserving their original quality.
